*01:27 PM, Saturday, March 21, 2026*

Elections to the Legislature of the south Indian state of Tamil Nadu   has been announced by the  Election Commission of India. Tanil Nadu will go to the polls on 23 April 2026 to elect 234 members to it’s Legislative assembly, Counting of votes would commence on May 4th 2026, Tamil Nadu voters  number  approximately  5.67 Crores.Of these  2.77 crore  are males and 2.89 crore are female .There are 7,617 transgender voters.

India is our closest neighbour and within India, the southern state of Tamil Nadu with a 72 million population is the closest Indian state to Sri Lanka. Both Tamil Nadu and Sri Lanka are somewhat intertwined historically and geographically.

In recent times, political developments in Tamil Nadu have had much impact on Sri Lanka. Likewise happenings in Sri Lanka arouse much interest in  Tamil Nadu also. Political developments in Tamil Nadu are therefore of great  importance to Sri Lanka.

Furthermore popular Tamil actor Vijay who is the founder-leader of the Thamizhaga Vettrik Kazhagam(TVK) is also in the electoral fray.  He has announced that his party will be facing the elections alone without aligning with other political parties.Vijay who has a very large fan following in Sri Lanka is also married to  Sri Lanka  born Sangeetha.

It is  is against this backdrop that this column focuses on the forthcoming election in Tamil Nadu with particular emphasis on Vijay/.

Let me commence  by re-visiting the previous Tamil Nadu state election held in 2021.Since 1967  Tamil Nadu state  has been ruled by political parties subscribing to what is termed as Dravidian ideology. Either the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) or its alternative the All India Anna-Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK) have been enjoying power in the State alternately.

The  2021 Elections too conformed to this pattern. The DMK led by Muthuvel Karunanidhi  Stalin defeated the AIADMK led by Edappaadi K. Palaniswamy/The AIADMK   had ruled the State from 2011 onwards for ten years.

At the 2021 poll,the DMK led Secular Progressive Alliance (SPA) won 159 seats while the AIADMK led National Democratic Alliance (NDA) won 75. No other party or alliance won even a single seat.  The Naam Thamizhar Katchi (NTK) led by cinema director cum actor S. Seemaan contested all 234 seats and came third by polling over three million votes even though it failed to get an MLA elected.

Nominations for the 2026 elections  will close on April 7th 2026.  Details of the electorates contested by various political parties and the candidates fielded by them would be officially known only then. A detailed incisive analysis would be possible only after that.

However a preliminary overview indicates that the 2026  poll would be mainly on the lines of the 2021 election. The  Tamil Nadu election is very much likely be a battle between the DMK led SPA alliance and the AIADMK led NDA alliance. Yet the election is termed by  several  observers  as a four cornered fight because two other parties are also contesting  all 234 seats separately. One is the NTK led by Seemaan and the other is the TVK led by Vijay.

Some analysts view the election as a triangular tussle between the DMK led alliance,the AIADMK alliance and Vijay’s TVK. Seemaan’s NTK is not taken into account by these analysts despite the fact that the party polled 8% of the votes ib the  2021 poll and came third.

The TVK led by Vijay  has not contested any election  before, It is an untested, unproven political party. Yet there is much media hype about the party. Sections of the media  project the TVK as a potential winner at best and a “disruptor”at worst. Several opinion polls and surveys have been conducted.Predictably the results  are generally favourable to the TVK.It is also a fact that thousands of people mainly fans gather at meetings addressed by Vijay.  In a tragic incident  41 persons were killed some weeks ago in a stampede at Karur where Vijay addressed a rally.

Though well established political parties who have been doing politics for many years and senior political leaders with great political experience are facing the hustings,it is the novice Vijay and his fledgling TVP who are basking  most in the media limelight. This is due to two reasons.One is the filmstar’s personal popularity. The other is the yearning among sections of the people for an end to 59 years of Dravidian party rule in the state. Whatever the final outcome  of the April 23rd poll, there is no denying that Vijay’s political  entry  and the  launching of the  TVK  has  brightened the political skies of Tamil Nadu.

Joseph Vijay Chandrasekhar known to the world at large as Vijay is arguably the current  super star of Tamil cinema/. Although stylish veteran actor Rajinikanth has been acknowledged for long as Tamil cinema’s solitary super star, that position  seems to have changed in recent times. In terms of popularity and marketability, Vijay who celebrated his 51st birthday last year on 22nd June is way above Rajini who celebrated his 75th birthday last year on  December 12th.

Sangeetha- Chuzhipuram.

Vijay’s parents are the well-known film director SA Chandrasekhar and the reputed singer Shobha Chandrasekhar.Vijay who has a huge fan following in Sri Lanka is married to a Sri Lankan Tamil woman hailing from Chuzhipuram in Jaffna named Sangeetha Sornalingam.  She is a UK citizen now.They have a son Jason Sanjay and daughter Dhivya Sasha.

Vijay and Sangeetha were married ceremonially according to Hindu custom  in Chennai 27 years ago  on 25th August  1999. Before that,the couple registered their marriage formally in the UK on 10th July 1998. Sangeetha has filed for Divorce in February this year.

Vijay debuted in films as a child actor in 1984 in the film “Vetri”. He played lead actor for the first time  in the film “Naalaiya Theerppu” in 1992 at the age of 18. Thereafter he has acted in 69 films most of them block busters.Vijay is the highest paid actor in Tamil cinema today reportedly earning around 200 Crore Indian rupees per film. He is also the biggest box-office draw among Tamil actors.

Tamil Nadu has a long tradition of film personalities entering politics and forming their own parties. Some even became chief ministers. Former Chief Ministers CN Annadurai and M. Karunanidhi were renowned film script writers. Among the Tamil film actors who entered films are NS Krishnan, MR Radha, KR Ramasamy, MG Ramachandran (MGR), Sivaji Ganesan, SS Rajendran, VN Janaki, Jayalalithaa Jayaram, TR Rajendar, SV Shekhar, Napoleon, Vijayakanth, Seemaan, Sarathkumar, Radhika, Kamal Haasan and Udayanidhi Stalin. Even the current chief minister  MKStalin has acted in a few films and TV serials.

Vijay is therefore the latest in a long line of Tamil film actors transforming themselves into politicians.. He launched his own political party named “Thamizhaga Vettrik Kazhagam” (TVK) on 2nd February 2024. . Vijay’s fan clubs numbering around 85,000 were converted into party branches. The TVK claims a party membership of several millions.

As stated earlier there are two  peculiar aspects in the politics of Tamil Nadu. One is the continuous rule of Dravidian political parties. Two regional parties namely the DMK and the AIADMK  have been governing the state alternately since 1967. The other is the influence of films and film personalities in the political sphere.

Dravidian party dominance in Tamil Nadu and the influence of cinema in Tamil Nadu politics are both inter-related issues. In a sense the rise of Dravidian politics and growing influence of cinema on politics were processes that complemented each other.

The fact that a leading actor like Vijay could launch his own political party and aspire to be the future chief minister of Tamil Nadu is mainly due to the “cinematic politics” ecosystem prevailing in the state.

It could be seen therefore that the rise of Dravidian political parties in Tamil Nadu greatly helped enhance the influence of cinema in the State’s political sphere. Actor Vijay’s political entry is in keeping with this tradition. However Vijay and his TVK regard the DMK  and the AIADMK as their political opponents and the BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party) as their ideological adversary.

According to knowledgeable political observers Vijay’s TVK draws its support mainly from youths who are below 35. There is  much woman support also. In terms of area, Vijay’s support is more in urban areas than rural areas. The TVP is expected  to do well in the state capital Chennai as well as other Tamil Nadu cities.

On another level  Vijay is expected to get substantial support from the Vellala community, scheduled castes,linguistic minorities  and the religious  minorities. Vijay himself is a Vellala Christian.

Both the DMK and AIADMK form  multi-party alliances to contest elections. However after winning,they form a single party administration. Electoral allies are not given ministerial portfolios. This has led to some rumblings of discontent among other parties.

When Vijay launched his Party,he invited other political parties to align with him. Vijay promised them a share in power and Governance.In short , electoral allies would be given minister posts. Negotiations were conducted quietly with many political parties except the ruling DMK.This was because Vijay was positioning his TVK as the alternative to the DMK. Despite  offering minister posts,no Tamil Nadu party was willing to climb aboard Vijay’s bandwagon.

This reportedly was due to two reasons. One was that political parties were not willing to throw in their lot with the untested,unproven new kid on the block. The second was Vijay’s insistence that he alone should be the chief minster. Unable to secure any ally, Vijay made a virtue of necessity by announcing last week that the party would go it alone at the elections.

Though Vijay’s supporters project their idol-leader as the next chief minister,many analysts doubt this. Vijay’s TVK is expected to get about 10% to 20% percent of the  votes and win a handful of seats in the urban areas. This would make Vijay a major player in Tamil Nadu politics but not a winner. However the votes garnered by the TVK may affect the overall election results and be the determining factor in the election. Vijay’s party is expected to entice youth and woman voters from almost every party in the state.

Whatever these electoral calculations may be, a recent , unexpected turn of events has caused a major upheaval. A personal matter  concerning Vijay is creating much controversy. Since this personal issue has political ramifications, it is necessary to delve into this “ domestic discord”  matter briefly.

As stated earlier, Vijay’s wife is Sangeetha Sornalingam a British national of Sri Lankan Tamil origin. In recent times there have been many rumours and “gossip”news items about both being estranged. It was alleged that an extra-marital relationship between Vijay and another actress was the reason.

These rumours were proven true  when Sanggetha filed for divorce in February 2025. The following  excerpt from the “Indian Express”of 27th Feb 2026 stated as follows –

“A divorce petition has been filed before the District Court at Chengalpattu seeking dissolution of marriage between Sangeetha Vijay and actor-politician Vijay (Joseph Vijay Chandrasekhar) under Sections 27(1)(a), (b), (d) read with Sections 36 and 37 of the Special Marriage Act, 1954.”

“In her petition, Sangeetha, states that the marriage was first registered in the United Kingdom on July 10, 1998. A ceremonial marriage was later solemnised on August 25, 1999, at Rajah Muthiah Mandram, Egmore, Chennai, according to Hindu customs and rites.”

“The petition states that the early years of the marriage were “cordial and harmonious,” with the petitioner devoting herself to the household and upbringing of the children.”

“However, it alleges that in April 2021, she discovered that  Vijay was involved in an adulterous relationship with an actress, causing her “deep emotional pain and mental suffering, amounting to betrayal and violation of marital trust.”

“Though Vijay initially assured her that the relationship would end, the petition alleges that the association continued, leading to “mental cruelty,” public humiliation arising from social media posts, emotional neglect and constructive desertion.”

“It further states that efforts between September 2021 and February 2022, and again between August 2024 and February 2025, through counsels and personal discussions, failed to result in an amicable settlement.”

“Describing the marriage as having been “irretrievably broken” and surviving only on paper, the petitioner seeks a decree of dissolution, permanent alimony commensurate with the respondent’s income and social standing, the right to reside in the matrimonial home in Neelankarai until disposal of the petition or provision of equivalent accommodation, and conduct of proceedings in camera to protect the dignity and privacy of all parties .”

When news  about Sangeetha seeking divorce became public knowledge through the media,Vijay and his top party officials kept mum but Vijay’s supporter minions went to town on social media. Sangeetha was attacked and vilified mercilessly. She was accused of being a DMK stooge and puppet who was making false allegations against the “paragon of Virtue”Vijay.It was a DMK hatched plot to spoil Vijay’s electoral prospects, it was alleged.

The attacks on Sangeetha were so crude and vulgar that reputed women’s  organizations in India issued statements condemning the anti_Sangeetha campaign. When  his wife of 27 years and mother of his two children was being viciously attacked, Vijay kept silent. He dd not try to restrain or stop  his acolytes.

Subsequently Sangeetha filed an interim petition  before the Chengelpattu court in the first week of March seeking maintenance and residential rights amid their pending divorce case. According to Indian media reports  Sangeetha has requested the court to direct Vijay to pay appropriate maintenance until the divorce case is resolved.In addition to maintenance, Sangeetha has also sought the right to reside in Vijay’s property in Neelankarai, Chennai. She has asked the court to allow her to stay at the house located at Kapaleeswarar Nagar, Neelankarai, through an interim order.

It is noteworthy that Sangeetha in  the  divorce  petition had referred to her husband’s alleged adulterous relationship with another actress but had pointedly refrained from naming  her or citing her as a third party.  However in a bizarre twist Vijay and the popular Tamil actress Trisha Krishnan travelled together in the same vehicle to attend the wedding of the son of a film producer. Vijay clad in verti and Trisha wearing a saree were dressed in identical colours.

It appeared that Vijay and Trisha were conveying a message to the world in general  and Sangeetha in particular by this brazen act. The  controversial incident created a furore. For the first time several  persons in the cinema sphere condemned Vijay. While most of  Vijay’s supporters and defenders were struck dumb, a few even criticized him openly. Interestingly the attacks on Sangeetha by Vijay’s supporters lost steam and began drastically declining.

Despite its high literacy rate and industrialization level, Tamil Nadu is basically conservative with old fashioned values and norms. Yet  several prominent politicians and popular filmstars  have had more than ‘one wife”. In all these the first or legal wife has not complained publicly or sought a divorce. Sangeetha seeking a divorce from Vijay goes against this norm. It must not be forgotten that Sangeetha is a Jaffna Tamil from Sri Lanka.

It remains to be seen as to how the people of Tamil Nadu  especially the women  react to Vijay’s conduct when they vote on election day.Meanwhile Vijay and Sangeetha have been summoned by court for a prelimary hearing on April 20th. Three days later on April 23rd Tamil Nadu will go to the polls.
