The most explicit message of the GGI is that the leading status of the UN must be upheld, not challenged; the central role of the UN must be strengthened, not weakened.

The UN is not perfect, but without the UN, the world would only be worse. Creating parallel structures outside the UN or, worse still, putting together various exclusive blocs and circles is unpopular and unsustainable.

The most unequivocal requirement of the GGI is that the UN must keep pace with the times, improve its governance system through reform, and adapt to the evolving international situation and the changing dynamics in the global balance of power in the 21st century.
