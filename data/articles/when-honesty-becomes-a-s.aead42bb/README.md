Leaving Your Critics Nowhere to Go

If you had asked most PR professionals whether his reputation was recoverable, the honest answer would have been no. In PR, an apology from your enemy is the closest thing to a miracle. It almost never happens. And it does not happen because someone managed the situation well. It happens because someone told the truth so completely that there was nothing left to attack. In communications, we often talk about authenticity. The truth is that genuine authenticity is rare because it requires vulnerability. What Hunter Biden demonstrated was that radical honesty can sometimes be more persuasive than any reputation management strategy. When people feel they are hearing the truth without calculation behind it, the critical gaze softens. Truth is still enough to stop people in their tracks.

They buy on the basis of what they believe the company will become.
